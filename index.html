Five Es evaluation — study level

Economy
Good. Inputs were efficiently sourced; community mobilisation leveraged existing regional networks at low marginal cost. No evidence of over-resourcing relative to study scope.
Efficiency
Excellent. 320 households surveyed within budget. Water testing and community notification integrated into a single fieldwork round, minimising duplication.
Effectiveness
Good. 52% uptake of protective action among notified households is above the sector average for environmental health interventions of this type.
Cost-effectiveness
Good. BCR above 1.0 under base-case assumptions. Both benefit streams contribute positively. The chronic CRC stream drives most long-run value.
Equity
Adequate. The survey targeted a geographically defined community, not a population-stratified sample. Māori and Pacific household exposure was not separately analysed. A follow-up distributional analysis stratified by ethnicity and household income is recommended for future iterations. Te Tiriti obligations require that equity impacts on Māori be explicitly considered in study design.

Evaluative judgement

This investment is Good on cost-effectiveness (Five Es criterion 4). The discounted benefits across both health outcome streams exceed the $76,000 study cost under base-case assumptions, producing a positive NPV and BCR above 1.0. To improve VFI, the study should commission a follow-up equity analysis stratified by ethnicity and household income, and establish a standardised method for quantifying the chronic colorectal cancer risk reduction benefit stream in future iterations of this study design.
